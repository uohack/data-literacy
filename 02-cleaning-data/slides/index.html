CSV

- Spreadsheet in a text format
- The simplest you can get a spreadsheet
- CSV stands for comma separated values
- You can open CSVs in any spreadsheet application or a plain text editor
- You can also export a CSV from nearly every DB or spreadsheet app

Percent change

- Calculate the change of one value to another
- Used for all sorts of things, usually when comparing the same value over two time periods.
- Example:
> Total budget from last year versus this year
> Difference in sales between last month and this
> Compare how much you spent on food last month versus this month

Example

So, this is a very common task, you are assigned to analyze a proposed budget, in this case, the City of Eugene. So you get the proposed budget, a public document and it's 209 pages long. I'm going to give you a huge hint, the first important step to analyzing any budget is to figure out how the money is distributed at the top level. From there you can dig down as needed. In this case you can find that on page 62 of the PDF, although it's labeled as page 56. Your task is to figure out if there is anything interest at the top level of this budget.
